## Overview

Scout AI is an AI-powered assistant built into <Tooltip content="The research operations platform that hosts Scout AI and your project data.">UserLens</Tooltip>. It analyzes session notes, generates reports, and accelerates your research synthesis workflow. Scout AI can identify patterns, extract quotes, validate hypotheses, and draft deliverables — while keeping you in control of the output.

### What Scout AI can do

| Capability | Description |
|---|---|
| **Pattern detection** | Automatically identify recurring themes across sessions |
| **Quote extraction** | Surface notable quotes tagged by sentiment |
| **Hypothesis linking** | Find evidence that supports or refutes your hypotheses |
| **Ask Scout** | Ask natural language questions about your research data |
| **Summary generation** | Create session summaries, cluster descriptions, and briefings |
| **Report drafting** | Generate findings reports from your synthesis data |

---

## Getting started

### Access Scout AI

1. Open any project in UserLens.
2. Navigate to the **Scout AI** panel in the right sidebar.
3. The **Chat** tab opens by default.

### Data Scout AI uses

Scout AI analyzes data from your current project only:

- **Session notes** — Documents containing participant observations, quotes, and task performance data
- **Synthesis notes** — Your tagged observations and insights
- **Hypotheses** — Research hypotheses you have created in the **Synthesis** tab

:::tip[Better results]
The more structured and complete your project data, the more accurate Scout AI's analysis will be. See [Best practices](#best-practices) for guidance on preparing your data.
:::

## Tabs and features

Scout AI is organized into five tabs, each serving a different purpose.

### Chat tab

The Chat tab provides a conversational interface for asking questions about your research data.

1. Enter a question in the input field at the bottom of the panel.
2. Press **Enter** or select **Send**.
3. Scout AI responds based on your session notes and synthesis data.

**Example questions:**

- "Summarize the key findings from my sessions."
- "What are the main pain points users mentioned?"
- "What patterns do you see across participants?"
- "Which hypotheses are supported by the research?"

:::tip[Chat tips]
- Scout AI retains conversation context, so you can ask follow-up questions without repeating yourself.
- Select **Copy** on any response to copy it to your clipboard.
- Select **Save as Document** to save a response directly to your project's **Documents** tab.
- Select **New Chat** to start a fresh conversation and clear the current context.
:::

---

### Sessions tab

The Sessions tab generates summaries and analysis from your session notes.

**Before you begin:** Ensure your project's **Documents** tab contains at least one session note.

1. Select an output format from the dropdown.
2. Select **Generate Summary** (or **Generate Findings Report** for the Findings Report format).
3. Review the output, then select **Copy** or **Save as Document**.

**Available output formats:**

| Format | Best for |
|---|---|
| **Overview Summary** | Quick executive summary with key findings |
| **Detailed Analysis** | Participant-by-participant breakdown with severity ratings |
| **Theme Extraction** | Thematic analysis with patterns and relationships |
| **Key Quotes & Insights** | Impactful quotes grouped by theme with sentiment |
| **Findings Report** | Comprehensive report with full template structure |

### Artifacts tab

The Artifacts tab generates common UX research deliverables from your project data.

<Tabs queryString="artifact" defaultValue="personas">

<TabItem value="personas" label="Personas">

Generates user personas based on your research data.

Select **Generate Personas** to produce output that includes:

- Name, age, and occupation
- Background and context
- Goals and motivations
- Pain points and frustrations
- Behaviors and preferences
- A representative quote
- Relevant scenarios


</TabItem>

<TabItem value="journey-map" label="Journey map">

Creates a customer journey map with emotional highs and lows.

Select **Generate Journey Map** to produce output that includes:

- Journey phases and stages
- User actions at each stage
- Touchpoints and channels
- Emotional journey (thoughts and feelings)
- Pain points per stage
- Opportunities for improvement


</TabItem>

<TabItem value="theme-extraction" label="Theme extraction">

Extracts and analyzes themes across your qualitative data.

Select **Extract Themes** to produce output that includes:

- 3–7 major themes with descriptions
- Supporting evidence from sessions
- Theme frequency and prevalence
- Contradictions or outliers
- Theme relationships


</TabItem>

<TabItem value="hypothesis-linking" label="Hypothesis linking">

Analyzes your research data against existing hypotheses to find supporting or contradicting evidence.

**Prerequisites:**
- At least one hypothesis created in your **Synthesis** tab
- Session notes or synthesis data in your project

Select **Analyze Hypotheses** to produce output that includes, for each hypothesis:

- Current status vs. recommended status
- Confidence level (High, Medium, or Low)
- Supporting evidence with citations
- Contradicting evidence with citations
- Analysis explanation
- Suggested next steps

A **Summary** section also provides:

- Counts of validated, invalidated, partial, and untested hypotheses
- Key insights across all hypotheses
- Prioritized recommendations

:::tip[Hypothesis linking tips]
- Write clear, testable hypotheses. "Users struggle with onboarding" is more useful than "The app has problems."
- Mark the current status of each hypothesis accurately — Scout AI uses this to provide meaningful recommendations.
- Review contradicting evidence closely. Conflicting data often reveals important nuances.
:::


</TabItem>

<TabItem value="interview-guide" label="Interview guide">

Output includes:

- Wrap-up questions
- Closing script
- Tips for the interviewer

## Working with output

### Copy to clipboard

Every Scout AI output includes a **Copy** button. Select it to copy the content to your clipboard for use in other applications.

### Save as document

Select **Save as Document** to save output directly to your project:

1. Enter a document title in the dialog.
2. Select **Save Document**.

The document appears in your project's **Documents** tab.

### Follow-up suggestions

After generating output, Scout AI suggests relevant follow-up actions. For example, after generating personas, Scout AI might suggest:

- "Create journey map"
- "Find supporting quotes"
- "Identify persona pain points"

Select any suggestion to continue your analysis.

### Output history

Recent outputs appear at the bottom of the Scout AI panel. Select any item to view it again.

## Understanding citations

Scout AI cites evidence from your session notes using bracketed references. These citations help you trace insights back to their source data for verification.

| Citation format | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `[P1]` | Participant 1 |
| `[Session P2]` | Session notes for Participant 2 |
| `[Session Notes - P3]` | Session notes for Participant 3 |

:::caution[Verify citations]
Always check that cited evidence matches the original session notes before including it in a deliverable. Scout AI can misattribute or misrepresent source material.
:::

## Best practices

### Prepare your data

- **Use a consistent session note format** — Include participant identifiers, task performance, observations, and direct quotes.
- **Tag quotes clearly** — Use quotation marks and attribute each quote to a specific participant.
- **Document task success** — Note whether each task succeeded, failed, or partially completed.

### Get better results

- **Be specific in your questions** — "What did participants say about the checkout flow?" yields better results than "Tell me about the app."
- **Provide context** — Mention specific features, user types, or scenarios you want to analyze.
- **Use follow-up questions** — Build on Scout AI's responses to dig deeper into a topic.
- **Verify citations** — Confirm that cited evidence matches the original session notes before using it in a deliverable.

## Troubleshooting

### "No session notes found"

Scout AI could not identify session notes in your project. Ensure your documents meet at least one of the following conditions:

- The document title includes the words "session" and "note."
- The document contains session note indicators such as participant info, task completion data, and direct quotes.

### "No hypotheses found"

The Hypothesis Linking feature requires at least one hypothesis. Create hypotheses in your project's **Synthesis** tab before using this feature.

### Response seems incomplete

For long or complex analyses, Scout AI might truncate output. Try the following:

- Ask for a specific section (for example, "Show me only the top 5 findings").
- Break your request into smaller, focused parts.
- Select a more targeted template or output format.

### API errors

If you see "Failed to fetch" or a similar error:

- Check your internet connection.
- Confirm the API is available — contact your administrator if the issue persists.
- Wait a few moments, then try again.

- Your research data is sent to the AI service for processing.
- Outputs are not stored externally unless you explicitly save them.
